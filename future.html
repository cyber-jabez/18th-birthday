Page Seven

Chapter 18

Your story begins here.

18 is the first page of a chapter you get to write yourself. No curriculum. No one else's timeline. Just yours.

Now

The Beginning

Right now, at 18, everything is still open. College. New cities. New people. New versions of yourself. The slate isn't blank — it's full of who you already are. That's a better starting point.

College

Building Things

Civil engineering isn't just a degree — it's a way of thinking. You're learning to look at the world and ask: how does this hold together? How could it be stronger? That question will serve you in ways that go far beyond structures.

The Road

Somewhere New

One day you'll go somewhere you've never been. On a bike, or a train, or a plane, or on foot. And you'll look at an unfamiliar horizon and realize: this is yours too. The world gets bigger the more you move through it.

Becoming

More Yourself

Every year after this one you'll understand yourself a little better. What you actually want. What you can let go of. Who you want around. The growing up doesn't stop at 18 — it just gets more intentional.

The Work

Things You'll Build

Bridges, maybe. Roads. Foundations. Or other things — friendships that outlast distance, habits that hold you together, a life that looks like the one you actually wanted. Building is what you do. Let yourself do it.

Always

The Things That Stay

Whatever changes — careers, cities, routines — some things stay. The music that knows your mood. The rain that feels like yours. The purple corner of the world that is unmistakably you. Those aren't going anywhere. Neither is who you are.

The future doesn't ask you to be ready.
It just asks you to show up.

You've been doing that since day one.
